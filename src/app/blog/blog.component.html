Full FULL Stack

It seems like every developer and their mother is a 'Full Stack Developer' and I have a bone to pick with that term. The implication behind the term is that the developer can basically create an application from start to finish by themselves. They can handle the front and back end, great.

But what about the hardware and hosting?

Most full stack developers still require hosting services, like vercel, which I'm using to host this. That fine- its very easy. But its not realllly full stack is it? I personally want to eventually host this portfolio site on my own server, on my own network, with one of those sick LED infested homelabs. Of course this is a lot of work and opens up a lot of vulnerabilities. If I host this on my own network and do it wrong, im inviting in bad actors into my home. It would also take a lot of time for the price of the server equipment to compare to the hosting fees.

Surely there are some upsides to this. I mean, it is a learning opportunity. You can have direct access to your server with no middle man charging you a fee. You can be directly responsible for your uptime, and can customize it in ways that might not be possible with a hosting service. I'm sure I will discover more benefits as I continue this journey.

Right now I have made a little progress. I got an old computer for free from a family member and installed Ubuntu Server on it. After hours of troubleshooting a faulty driver I was able to get it connected to my network and accessed it from laptop via SSL. I'm not really sure what to do next.
